THESIS ABSTRACT

Functional Insights into Nup43: A Nup107 Complex Member Influencing Locomotory Behaviour and Longevity of Drosophila melanogaster

One of the most extensively studied nucleoporin subcomplexes is the Nup107–160 subcomplex. Nucleoporin 43 (Nup43) is a conserved component of this complex, positioned on its short arm, and is maintained across higher eukaryotes. Despite its high level of conservation, Nup43 remains relatively understudied, with no prior functional investigations reported in Drosophila melanogaster. This study investigates the role of Nup43 in neurobehavioural functions, with a particular focus on locomotory defects in D. melanogaster.

Homozygous Nup43 mutants exhibited significant locomotory impairments, including defects in both climbing and crawling behaviour. To assess the neurobehavioural consequences, we examined larval crawling speed and found a notable reduction in mutants, which could be rescued by inducible expression of Nup43 using the Gal4/UAS system in both ubiquitous and tissue-specific contexts. This result strongly suggests that Nup43 or its interactors are essential for normal neurobehaviour. Furthermore, we observed that knocking out hdac6 in the Nup43 null background rescued the crawling defects, indicating a potential interaction between these two proteins. Lifespan analysis of Nup43 mutants revealed a significant reduction in longevity, further emphasizing the importance of Nup43 in organismal health. Additionally, overexpression of Nup43 in germline cells successfully rescued male sterility, restoring normal spermatogenesis. Overall, our findings highlight the critical role of Nup43 in locomotory behaviour, lifespan, and fertility in Drosophila, with potential implications for understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying neurobehavioural and reproductive disorders.
